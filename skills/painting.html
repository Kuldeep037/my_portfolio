Artist Statement

My artistic practice explores the intersection between the natural world and human experience. As a robotics engineer by profession, I find that painting provides a necessary counterbalance to the precision and logic of my technical work.

My landscapes capture moments of tranquility and majesty in nature, while my abstract works delve into the emotional experiences that connect us to our environment. I'm particularly interested in how color and composition can evoke specific emotional responses.

"I paint to understand the world differently than I do through equations and algorithms. The brush allows me to explore questions that don't have binary answers."

Watercolor is my primary medium for its transparency and unpredictability. I embrace the way water moves pigment in sometimes unexpected directions â€” a reminder that not everything can or should be controlled. When working with acrylics, I focus on building textured layers that invite viewers to consider both what's visible and what lies beneath.

My engineering background influences my artistic approach in subtle ways: I find myself drawn to certain structural patterns in nature, and I often analyze the interplay of light and shadow with a somewhat technical eye. Yet painting allows me to transcend the boundaries of technical precision and enter a space where intuition guides creation.

Artistic Journey

2018
First Watercolor Workshop

Participated in a weekend workshop that introduced me to basic watercolor techniques. This sparked my interest in pursuing painting as a serious hobby.

2019
Local Art Group Membership

Joined a local art group that meets monthly for plein air painting sessions. Learning from experienced artists significantly improved my understanding of composition and color theory.

2020
First Exhibition

Participated in a group exhibition at a community gallery. The experience of sharing my work publicly was both nerve-wracking and thrilling.

2021
Advanced Techniques Study

Completed an online course focused on advanced acrylic techniques and mixed media approaches, which opened new avenues for experimentation in my work.

2022
Solo Exhibition

Held my first solo exhibition titled "Natural Algorithms" at a local arts center, exploring the connections between nature, technology, and art.

2023
Art Retreat

Attended a week-long painting retreat in the mountains, which deepened my connection to landscape painting and inspired a new series of works.
